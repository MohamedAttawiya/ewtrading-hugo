    <h2>Alkyd Families</h2>

    <div class="grid three">
      <article class="card">
        <h3>Long Oil Alkyd</h3>
        <p class="muted">
          High oil content (~56&ndash;70%). Flexible films, excellent gloss and retention.
          Typical uses: exterior enamels, wood, marine.
        </p>
        <ul class="chips" role="list">
          <li>Exterior enamels</li>
          <li>Wood varnishes</li>
          <li>Marine</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <h3>Medium Oil Alkyd</h3>
        <p class="muted">
          Balanced dry, hardness and flexibility (~46&ndash;55%).
          Typical uses: architectural enamels, varnishes, interior/exterior.
        </p>
        <ul class="chips" role="list">
          <li>Architectural</li>
          <li>General purpose</li>
          <li>Varnishes</li>
        </ul>
      </article>

      <article class="card">
        <h3>Short Oil Alkyd</h3>
        <p class="muted">
          Low oil (&lt;46%). Fast-drying, hard films.
          Typical uses: primers, industrial enamels, corrosion-resistant systems.
        </p>
        <ul class="chips" role="list">
          <li>Primers</li>
          <li>Industrial</li>
          <li>Fast dry</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
    </div>

    <div class="grid two mt-1">
      <article class="card">
        <h3>Urethane-Modified Alkyd</h3>
        <p class="muted">
          Alkyds with urethane groups for higher hardness, chemical resistance and durability.
          Typical uses: parquet varnishes, floor coatings, automotive finishes.
        </p>
        <ul class="chips" role="list">

          <li>Automotive</li>
